What is ISTQB, Why you Should do it from Uncodemy?

ISTQB stands for the International Software Testing Qualifications Board. It's a non-profit organization that provides certifications for software testing professionals worldwide. The certifications are designed to demonstrate a candidate's knowledge and skills in software testing, and they're widely recognized in the industry. Some of the certifications offered by ISTQB include:

Foundation Level Certification: This is the entry-level certification and covers basic concepts of software testing.

Advanced Level Certification: This certification is more advanced and focuses on specific areas of testing such as Agile testing, security testing, and test management.

Expert Level Certification: This is the highest level of certification offered by ISTQB and is meant for experienced professionals who have mastered the field of software testing.

    1. What is ISTQB Certification?

    ISTQB (International Software Testing Qualifications Board) Certification is a globally recognized credential in the field of software testing. It demonstrates that you have a solid understanding of the fundamental concepts of software testing, including principles, methods, and tools. The certification is designed to provide a common framework for testers to improve their skills and knowledge, and to establish a common language and understanding of testing concepts. The certification is divided into different levels, including Foundation Level, Advanced Level, and Expert Level, each with its own set of skills and knowledge requirements. So, in short, ISTQB Certification is a valuable asset for anyone working in software testing and a great way to validate your skills and knowledge in the field.
