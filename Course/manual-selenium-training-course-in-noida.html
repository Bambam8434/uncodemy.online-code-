What is Manual testing ? Why you should do it from Uncodemy ?

What will you gain?

Manual testing is a type of software testing where a human manually executes tests on a software application to identify any issues or defects. It is usually performed in an exploratory manner, where the tester follows a test plan to evaluate the functionality and usability of the software. The tester will usually interact with the software, enter data, and observe the results, then compare the results to the expected behavior to find any discrepancies. It's one of the oldest and most common forms of software testing, and it's still widely used today.

To help with understanding, let's break it down into a few main steps: - First, the tester will analyze the requirements and specifications of the software being tested. - Next, the tester will design test cases to cover all the functionality of the software. - The tester will then execute the test cases and observe the results. - Any issues or bugs found during the testing will be documented and reported to the development team. - Finally, the tester will verify whether the issues have been fixed and the software is functioning correctly.     11. What is the main concept of manual testing?

    At its core, manual testing is all about validating that a piece of software is working as it should. Manual testers need to have a good eye for detail, as they need to be able to spot even the smallest inconsistencies or errors in the software. Another important concept of manual testing is risk-based testing, which involves identifying and prioritizing the areas of the software that are most likely to have issues.

    12. What is the goal of manual testing?

    The goal of manual testing is to find any defects or errors in the software before it's released to the end user. This is important because it helps to ensure that the software is functioning as it should, and that any issues can be fixed before they cause problems for the user. Manual testing also helps to verify that the software meets the requirements of the customer or stakeholder. Finally, manual testing is also important for ensuring that the software is easy to use and meets the usability standards of the organization. Ultimately, the goal of manual testing is to improve the quality of the software.
